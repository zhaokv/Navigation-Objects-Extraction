USED, GOOD

BRING HOTEL STYLE TO YOUR APARTMENT -?FOR LESS THAN YOU THINK

By DAVID LAHUTA

Loading new images...

June 19, 2007 -- BOLSTERED by a record number of guests not seen since before 9/11, the hotel industry is expected to invest $5.5 billion on enhancements in 2007, according to Pricewaterhouse-Coopers? latest forecast.

Sleek flat screen televisions are replacing bulky tube models, outdated furniture is being updated and mattresses - some only two years old - are being replaced by newer, fluffier versions.

With so much demand for the new, it?s not surprising that some consumers are filling their homes with the old. But where does all that used furniture go? More and more, hotel liquidators across the country are buying the items in bulk (or getting paid to haul them out) then selling them to the public for up to 90 percent off the original price.

?We just pulled 900 Heavenly Beds from the Essex House in Manhattan,? says David White, owner of Universal Hotel Liquidators, an 80,000-square-foot warehouse in New Haven

that?s been reselling used hotel furniture since 1999.

?Brand new, those beds cost around $2,000. You can buy a queen-sized mattress from us for $200 and it?s only a couple of years old.?

Like other liquidation centers - including Hotel Surplus Outlet in Los Angeles, Fort Pitt Furniture in Chicago, and IRCA Hotel Services in Phoenix - Universal sells furniture individually or in sets for an even greater discount, like a recent package taken from Manhattan?s Rihga Royal Hotel, now known as the London NYC.

For $700, the set included a king-sized bed, armoire, night stands, lamps, sofa, reclining chair, coffee table, TV and stereo.

?The cool thing about the place is that the prices are excellent and a lot of what they sell is in really good condition,? says Peter Johns of Wallingford, Connecticut, who buys furniture from the New Haven store, refinishes it, then sells it for a profit. ?I even have a nightstand in my bedroom that?s from the old Hyatt Regency.?

You can expect to find a bit of everything in Universal?s gigantic warehouse: entire rooms filled with glass table-tops, others filled with lampshades, couches, banquet chairs, electronics, armoires and coffee tables. And considering the growing number of hotels eager to upgrade its amenities in the tri-state area, Universal?s inventory changes constantly.

Understandably, it?s a popular place for nearby Yale students in search of inexpensive furniture, but it?s also great for New Yorkers looking for a bargain.

?The only thing I don?t understand is when people say they?d never sleep on a used mattress or buy used furniture,? says White. ?What do you sleep on when you?re on vacation? It?s the exact same thing.?
